Achievements of Past PLS

Since the first Pastoralist Leadership Summit (PLS) hosted by Narok County in 2013, notable achievements have been realized, including convening and uniting the national and county-level pastoralist leadership to speak in one voice. The platform has become a vital currency and a shared leadership space for expressing and echoing Kenya's pastoralist voices and aspirations.

Some of the notable achievements of the PLS include the following: First, it has led the formation of institution development, including the creation of the Ministry of Development of Northern Kenya and other Arid Lands under the office of the Prime Minister and National Drought Management Authority (NDMA), National Council for Nomadic Education (NACONEK) and Investment fund and ASAL stakeholders’ forum. Major policy development areas are Sessional paper no 8 of 2012, ASAL policy, Vision 2023 annexe, legislation on the protection and registration of the customary lands - the Community Land Act 2016. Additionally, it has led to the creation of the Livestock Marketing Board and the legal framework for operations of the Equalization Funds. Notably, it has contributed to developing the National Drought Emergency Fund (NDEF) Regulations, 2021, for the national government to commit an annual budget of KES 2 billion Kenya Shillings to climate change and drought response activities in Kenya. These policies are meant to push the state to make deliberate policy provisions to close the history of marginalization and neglect of the region and initiate new national developments in pastoralist areas and the ASAL.

Second, PLS has influenced actions through PPG members' lobbying, advocating, and articulating issues that have contributed to restoring peace, ending cattle thefts, mitigating inter-community violence, and implementing appropriate policy measures in the pastoralist regions.

Third, following a resolution from the PLS, the County Governors agreed to allocate at least 10% of the county development budget to the livestock sector. This outcome further consolidated pastoralist leadership voice in public rejection of the Building Bridges Initiatives (BBI) as well as several adverse anti-pastoralist clauses and other areas of general national interest that include a clause that aimed at creating the ombudsman's office for the judiciary and biased revenue-sharing formula, that would have resulted in the majority of the FCDC counties losing revenues based on the principle of one-person, one shilling & one vote. The pastoralist leader's counterproposal was to moderate it with one kilometre, one shilling, and one vote; however, for equity and interest of national development, the public resources in Kenya should be shared based on one shilling, one nation, one people to harness potential wherever it is available in the country.

Finally, the PLS initiative resulted in the Government of Kenya partnering with the World Bank in 2018 to increase the region's investment portfolio and infrastructure development. The regional investment program was launched with over USD two billion, including projects such as Off Grid Solar Access, Climate Smart Agriculture, Water and Sanitation Development Project, Northeastern Transport Improvement Project, Development Response to Displacement Impacts and National Safety Net Program Additional Financing.

What to Expect

Over three days, 400+ Delegates will participate in different activities aligned cutting across the thematic tracks to devise and implement comprehensive strategies to mitigate and ultimately end drought emergencies, secure land rights for communities, address and solve inter-community conflicts, enhance livestock production, marketing, and overall livelihood for pastoralists in the drylands region.

Expected Outcomes

Develop comprehensive policy recommendations addressing key challenges pastoralist communities face. Establish an investment roadmap that outlines strategic interventions and policy reforms needed to support pastoralism.

Identify and analyze barriers hindering the effective implementation of pastoralist policies. Develop an intervention strategy to address these bottlenecks and enhance policy implementation.

Facilitate the development of a roadmap to accelerate the implementation of priority policies and investment options identified during the summit. Foster collaboration between national and county governments, private sector stakeholders, and development partners to ensure sustainable financing and implementation of pastoralist-focused initiatives.

Establish a sustainable funding mechanism that supports long-term development investments in pastoralist areas. Enhance financial structures and linkages between national and county levels to mobilize resources for pastoralist development projects.

How do we get there?

Address areas that require commitment from government for immediate actions:

  • Appoint the members of the Board of Directors to the National Livestock Development and Promotion Service as per the legal notice no 87 of 2nd June 2016.
  • Increase the annual budget allocation to two billion shillings to the drought contingency fund.
  • Strengthen the Establishment of Northern Kenya Investment Fund
  • Develop the carbon credit programs in the pastoralist areas.
  • Provide an adequate budget to the State Department of Land & Physical Planning to
  • Implement the Community Land Act 2016.
  • Sort out the Natural resource benefit-sharing policies and legislation.

Highlighting areas of strategic interventions to enhance the implementation of Vision 2030 and the critical foundation of development in the drylands of Kenya.

  • Preventing crime and justice promotes peace and security, particularly in ending the region's intercommunity conflicts, cattle theft, and violent extremism. The conference will highlight the significance of one standard: the rule of law, equal measures and the value of human life in Kenya.
  • Increase infrastructural development (telecommunication, roads, & energy - zero-mile connection.
  • To raise awareness of Human capital development, a national adult literacy campaign programme will be initiated to increase literacy levels in pastoralists.

These issues require adequate sensitization of the pastoralist political leadership to reach a consensus, achieve a clear understanding, settle debate, and fast-track actions.
